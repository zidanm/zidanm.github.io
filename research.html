Working Papers

“Understanding Regional Growth Heterogeneity” (Job Market Paper)

Abstract: This paper takes a new approach to understand the sources of regional growth heterogeneity by integrating model averaging and decomposition methods in order to understand how cross-country growth differences arise from differences in explanatory variables versus differences in coefficients. My approach allows the growth process of different economies to follow distinct processes and accounts for model uncertainty. I study growth rates between 1975-2014 for 130 countries grouped into 6 regions. My results reject the homogeneous coefficients assumption that is commonly used in the growth literature. Factors that appear important to explain growth heterogeneity in one region does not necessarily matter in other regions. Moreover, my findings indicate that regional dummies are insufficient to categorize regional parameter and growth heterogeneity.

"Foreign Aid and Economic Growth"

Abstract: This paper examines the foreign aid and economic growth relation using cross-country growth regressions and considers the choice of the measure of aid as a source of model uncertainty using model averaging methods. The preliminary results show that the choice of measurement can have a substantial impact on estimates and on the robustness of these estimates. The paper also aims to examine the identity of donor countries and the influence it may have on aid-growth relation and to address the issue of parameter heterogeneity in this context using decomposition methods.
